Q2 of the Stellar Community Fund Public Goods Award wrapped with **17 projects funded** and **more than USD 400,000** disbursed. For us, the milestone is simpler: this was the first round where award voting ran as **privacy-preserving on-chain governance on Stellar**, with tallies anyone can verify and individual ballots that stay secret.

{/* truncate */}

## What ran on Tansu

The award program uses a dedicated Tansu project on testnet ([testnet.tansu.dev](https://testnet.tansu.dev), `stellarpg` project). Proposals, vote commitments, and execution all land on-chain. Voters were weighted through the [Neural Quorum Governance](https://scf-public-goods-maintenance.github.io/public-goods-award/) (NQG) contract integration we built for this project — not the default badge table.

Anonymous voting followed the Pedersen commitment scheme we [documented last year](/blog/annonymous_voting): votes are encrypted client-side, commitments are stored on-chain, and maintainers upload tallies at execution time. The contract verifies the math before accepting the outcome. You get an auditable election without publishing who voted for what.

If you want the protocol-level detail, the award maintainers published [Tansu modifications for the program](https://scf-public-goods-maintenance.github.io/pg-award/tansu-modifications/) and [proposer instructions](https://scf-public-goods-maintenance.github.io/pg-award/proposer-instructions/). Intake numbers and funded project lists live in the [program repository](https://github.com/SCF-Public-Goods-Maintenance/scf-public-goods-maintenance.github.io).

## What we learned

Running a real funding round at this scale surfaced the usual sharp edges — wallet UX, weight edge cases around the NQG threshold, execution timing — but nothing that blocked the round. The collaboration with SDF Community (thank you **Anke Liu** and **Alex Olieman**) kept the process grounded in what pilots actually needed rather than what looked neat in a diagram.
